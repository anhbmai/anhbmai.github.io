GLITCH ART

Images glitched using data bending and data moshing.

This series explores the relationship between physical color and digital distortion. I started with close-up images of my eye makeup, using bright and layered colors as a base. I then intentionally corrupted the files using tools like TextEdit and Audacity, creating unpredictable glitch effects. These distortions interact with the original makeup in unexpected ways. At times, the colors align naturally, such as grey eyeshadow blending with grey glitch tones. These moments feel unplanned but meaningful. I refine the results in Photoshop, enhancing certain textures and color relationships while keeping the raw quality of the glitch. This work is an experiment in how real-world color transforms through digital processes, and how new forms of visual harmony can emerge from disruption.
